San Diego enjoys a mild Mediterranean climate with temperatures typically ranging from 65-80°F most of the year. Coastal areas benefit from the marine layer and cool ocean breezes, making air conditioning less dependent than inland areas. However, during summer heat waves and Santa Ana wind events, temperatures can spike significantly, making AC essential for comfort and safety. The city's diverse geography means climate needs vary from the cool coast to warmer inland valleys.

San Diego is California's second-largest city and one of the most desirable places to live in the United States. Known for its beautiful beaches, perfect weather, and diverse neighborhoods, San Diego offers everything from the bustling Gaslamp Quarter downtown to laid-back beach communities like Pacific Beach and Ocean Beach. The city has a strong military presence with Naval Base San Diego and Marine Corps bases, plus a growing biotech and tech industry. Housing ranges from historic craftsman homes in North Park to modern condos downtown and family homes throughout the suburbs.
